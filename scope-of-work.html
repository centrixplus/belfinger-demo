Executive Summary

Belfinger operates and maintains the Instrumentation and Control Systems (DCS) across all MEW Water Sector sites in the State of Kuwait under contract MEW/MC/6132/2024/2025. Today, maintenance work is coordinated on paper forms (Maintenance / Defect Sheets, Equipment Lists, Routine Maintenance Reports), routed by phone and WhatsApp, and reconciled manually at invoicing time.

The problems this creates

1 in 8
Forms lost or incomplete
~40h
Avg. invoice dispute cycle
0
SLA visibility for urgent jobs
~3×
Manual reconciliation effort
This document proposes a web + iPad-based Maintenance Management System that replaces the paper workflow end-to-end, preserving the exact roles, signatures, and approval stages already in use today — while delivering measurable improvements in response time, invoice accuracy, and audit confidence.

Expected outcomes

  • Zero lost reports — all checklists, photos, signatures captured digitally on the iPad
  • Faster SLA response — automated routing, real-time alerts, and escalation when urgent jobs stall
  • Fewer invoice disputes — photo evidence + audit trail on every line item
  • Single source of truth for all maintenance history, equipment status, and outstanding invoices
  • Bilingual operation (EN / AR) — matches the existing MEW forms
  • Offline-capable field work — engineers in basements and pump stations lose no data

Scope — In & Out

This is what we will build in Phase 1. Items listed as out-of-scope can be revisited in a future phase, priced separately.

✅ In Scope — Phase 1

Work Management

  • Two job types: PM (scheduled) + Issue Request (corrective)
  • Full approval chain: MEW → Belfinger → Contractor → Engineer
  • "Choose-who-to-send-back" rejection & rework logic
  • SLA tracking with automated escalation

Asset Register

  • Per-site equipment with Tag Numbers (e.g. LTD-1211)
  • Site → Sub-location hierarchy (D1–D12, Shuwaikh, Doha…)
  • Equipment types linked to checklist templates
  • Working / Defective / Under Repair status

Checklists & Reports

  • Typed checklists per equipment type
  • Calibration matrix with auto % error (0/25/50/75/100%)
  • Photos tagged to specific equipment
  • Dual digital signatures (Belfinger + MEW)
  • Free-form work reports for non-checklist jobs

Work Permits

  • First-class work-permit field on every WO
  • No-permit-no-work enforcement

Invoicing

  • BOQ master catalog (MEW pre-approved)
  • Unit price snapshotting
  • Versioned invoices with rework history
  • Auto-routing through approval chain

iPad Field App

  • Offline-first PWA (basements, pump stations)
  • QR / barcode tag scanning
  • Previous-reading prefill
  • Mandatory-field gate before submission

Platform

  • Automated PM scheduler (nightly, KW-holiday aware)
  • Email + in-app + SMS notifications
  • Bilingual EN / AR with RTL support
  • Append-only audit log for compliance

❌ Out of Scope — Phase 1

These items may be added in a future phase if required. They are excluded from the Phase 1 timeline and commercials.

  • MEW finance / GL integration. Invoices are exported as PDF for manual handover. Direct integration can be added in Phase 2 if MEW's finance system provides an API.
  • Subcontractor → Belfinger pricing. Handled offline in v1. Only the MEW-approved BOQ is in the system.
  • Predictive maintenance & analytics. Requires historical data first. Best revisited after 6+ months of operation.
  • Native iOS / Android apps. The PWA covers iPad and Android tablet without App Store review.
  • Real-time DCS / SCADA telemetry. Possible in Phase 2 once MEW's DCS APIs are documented.
  • Parts / inventory management. Phase 1 captures "spare installed" as a text field; full parts module (stock levels, PO, re-order points) is a future phase.
  • Financial accounting (GL, AR, AP). Use Belfinger's existing accounting system.

Commercial Options

Assumptions

The scope, timeline, and commercials in this document assume the following. If any assumption proves incorrect, timeline and / or cost may need to be revisited.

  • Belfinger provides the BOQ master catalog, list of sites and sub-locations, list of departments, PM cadences, equipment types and checklist templates.
  • MEW provides the list of MEW users (Requesters, Approvers) and confirms whether Requester and Approver are separate roles.
  • Kuwait public holidays for the scheduler are provided by Belfinger admin.
  • iPads are provided by Belfinger for site engineers. No App Store distribution required — the system runs on iPad Safari / Chrome as a PWA.
  • Subcontractor onboarding is coordinated by Belfinger — one named point of contact per subcontractor to provide their users, departments, and go-live schedule.
  • Data residency decision (cloud vs on-prem) is made by MEW before the start of Phase 3. Both paths are supported by the architecture.
  • Existing paper records from 2024–2025 are archival only; not migrated into the new system in Phase 1.
  • Email + SMS providers are agreed with Belfinger IT before Phase 7. Local KW provider preferred for SMS.

Open Questions

These need to be resolved before Phase 2 (Detailed Planning) begins:

  1. MEW Separation of Duties — is Requester and Approver one combined role, or two separate user types?
  2. Belfinger Manager cardinality — one person or multiple (round-robin / first-to-act / all-must-approve)?
  3. Attachments beyond photos — do we need PDFs on WOs (MEW's original defect report), signed forms, delivery notes for parts?
  4. SLA targets — Belfinger's numeric commitments per urgency per department (currently placeholder values).
  5. Kuwait public holidays — official list for calendar year 2026–2027.
  6. SMS provider — which KW carrier / gateway does Belfinger prefer?
  7. Hosting decision — cloud (AWS Bahrain) vs on-prem (Kuwait). Data residency requirements from MEW.
  8. Role gap check — is there an accountant / auditor / read-only viewer role we haven't captured?
